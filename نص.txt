Phonetics: is the study of speech sounds. It also studies the production and the perception of sounds.
 
•Phonology: is the study of the distribution of sounds in a language and the interactions between those different sounds.


•A Phoneme: is the smallest unit of sound which is significant in a language and could change the meaning of words.

•For example, the phoneme /l/ in a word like milk.

Allophones: the different realizations or pronunciations of a certain phoneme.
•When we are talking about such realizations, the phoneme is in slashes /…/ and the allophones are in square brackets […]
has the allophones [p] and [ph].

•A Minimal Pair is a pair of words that vary by only a single sound.

•/d/ and /z/ in wide and wise.
